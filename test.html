An Analysis of the Arkansas 911 Ecosystem

Maximizing the Return on Next Generation Investment

A System at a Critical Juncture

The Arkansas 911 system is a large, complex, and vital public service undergoing a period of intense transformation. As the state invests heavily in Next Generation 911 (NG911) infrastructure, understanding the scale of operations and the financial commitments involved is essential for strategic planning and ensuring a maximum return on investment.

The NG911 Transition: Building the Highway, Missing the On-Ramps

Arkansas has made remarkable progress in building the foundational "digital highway" for NG911—the statewide Emergency Services IP Network (ESInet). However, a closer look at core service readiness reveals a critical gap between network infrastructure and the ability of many Public Safety Answering Points (PSAPs) to handle the advanced data that this new highway was built to carry.

NG911 ESInet Connectivity

The state has successfully connected the vast majority of its PSAPs to the IP-based network, a monumental infrastructure achievement.

Core Services: i3 vs. Transitional Readiness

While network connectivity is high, readiness for the NENA i3 standard—essential for receiving rich data like video, health profiles, and precise location—is lagging. Many PSAPs remain on transitional systems, limiting the system's full potential and creating a gap in the return on investment. [4]

Foundational Cracks I: The GIS Data Deficit

Accurate location is the cornerstone of effective NG911. The system's ability to automatically and precisely route calls based on geography depends entirely on the quality of its Geographic Information System (GIS) data. Arkansas's own strategic plans acknowledge significant gaps in the foundational GIS layers needed for this critical function, creating an unreliable base for the entire NG911 structure.

Foundational Cracks II: The Human Element Under Pressure

The state's 911 system is facing a critical and persistent staffing shortage, placing immense strain on its most valuable asset: the public safety telecommunicators. This is not just an HR issue; it is an operational crisis with direct consequences for public safety, service quality, and fiscal health.

The Statewide Vacancy Crisis

A severe, statewide shortage of telecommunicators puts immense pressure on existing staff, increasing the risk of burnout and errors. This reflects a national trend where 82% of 911 centers report being understaffed. [7, 8]

This shortage forces mandatory overtime, increases stress, and contributes to a vicious cycle of high turnover. [9, 10, 11]

The Vicious Cycle of Burnout

The staffing shortage is both a cause and an effect of a challenging work environment. High stress and cognitive load from being understaffed are primary drivers of employee turnover, which in turn worsens the staffing shortage.

🏃‍♂️➡️🔥➡️🏃‍♀️

High Vacancies → Increased Overtime → Burnout → More Vacancies

This feedback loop degrades service quality, as seen in reports of 911 call delays in Little Rock, and makes recruitment even more difficult. [10, 12] Breaking this cycle requires tools that reduce dispatcher workload and stress.

The Operational Reality: Daily Hurdles on the Front Lines

The systemic challenges of data gaps and staffing shortages translate into tangible, moment-to-moment hurdles for dispatchers. These daily operational inefficiencies are not just minor frustrations; they are direct drivers of risk, delay, and inequity in emergency response.

Challenge: Language Barriers

📞 ➡️ ⏳ ➡️ 🗣️

Reliance on Third-Party Interpreters

Dialing out to a separate interpretation service introduces significant delays (often 60+ seconds), adds complexity, and increases the chance of miscommunication. This creates a two-tiered system where non-English speakers face inherent delays in receiving help.

Challenge: Manual Data Processing

🎧 ➡️ ⌨️ ➡️ 🤯

Cognitive Overload and Missed Keywords

Understaffed dispatchers must simultaneously listen, type, and identify critical keywords under immense pressure. This manual process risks missing vital details ("he has a gun," "I can't breathe") that could change an emergency outcome, especially when compounded by poor GIS data requiring manual location verification.

The Quantifiable Consequences: Measuring the Cost of Delay

The challenges facing Arkansas's 911 system have measurable and severe consequences. Delays caused by data gaps and operational hurdles translate directly into disparate response times, negative health outcomes, and significant, recurring financial costs to the state and its communities.

The Rural-Urban Response Divide

Vast geography and GIS data gaps create a significant disparity in how quickly help can arrive. National data shows average EMS response times in rural areas are more than double those in urban centers. [13]

The Path to a Safer Arkansas: An Intelligent Safety Platform as the Unifying Layer

An intelligent safety platform like RapidSOS provides a unified data layer that directly addresses Arkansas's core challenges. It acts as a force multiplier, enhancing the state's NG911 investment by delivering immediate, life-saving data to every PSAP, thereby closing the gap between infrastructure and operational capability.

The Challenge

Inaccurate Location & GIS Data Gaps

Reliance on incomplete GIS data and caller descriptions leads to response delays, especially in rural areas, undermining the core function of NG911. [5]

The RapidSOS Solution

Device-Based Location & Rich Data

Deliver precise, real-time GPS coordinates and additional data (health info, vehicle telematics) directly from the calling device, bypassing legacy location systems. [17, 18]

The Outcome

Faster, More Informed Response

Dramatically reduce response times, improve situational awareness for responders, and improve survival outcomes, as demonstrated in numerous save stories. [19, 20]

Dispatcher Overload & Burnout

High vacancy rates (145 statewide) and manual data entry increase stress, cognitive load, and the risk of errors, fueling a costly turnover cycle. [1, 9]

A Unified, Automated Platform

Integrate data into a single screen and automate data entry via CAD integration, reducing manual tasks, eliminating screen-switching, and lowering cognitive load. [17, 21, 22]

Improved Efficiency & Staff Wellness

Decrease call handling times, support dispatcher focus and well-being, and directly combat the primary drivers of burnout, helping to reduce costly staff turnover.

Language Barriers & Missed Details

Using third-party translation services causes critical delays, while manual listening under pressure risks missing vital keywords in a chaotic call.

Live Translation & AI Transcription

Provide instant, on-screen text translation and automatically transcribe calls, using AI to highlight keywords for immediate recognition and enhanced awareness. [17]

Equitable Access & Enhanced Safety

Serve all communities faster regardless of language, and ensure critical details are never missed, leading to safer and more effective resource allocation.
